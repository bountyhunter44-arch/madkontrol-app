# Debate Rules

## Core Rule

The platform exists to improve collective reasoning. Participants should be helped to become clearer, fairer, and more specific.

## Participant Rules

- State claims clearly.
- Distinguish facts, values, preferences, and predictions.
- Acknowledge uncertainty when evidence is weak.
- Respond to the strongest fair version of the opposing view.
- Avoid personal attacks.
- Let summaries preserve disagreement.
- Accept correction when a claim is shown to be unsupported.

## AI Participant Rules

- Always disclose AI role.
- Use phrases like "Based on the available context" and "I may be missing information" when appropriate.
- Ask before reframing a human's position in a strong way.
- Do not invent citations.
- Do not claim private lived experience.
- Do not treat compromise as always superior to disagreement.
- Do not perform false neutrality when evidence is asymmetric.

## Debate Modes

### Exploratory

Goal: understand a topic and map open questions.

### Deliberative

Goal: compare options and identify tradeoffs.

### Adversarial

Goal: test claims under pressure while preserving respect.

### Synthesis

Goal: produce summaries, overlap maps, and compromise candidates.

## Output Rules

Every public summary should include:

- Main claims.
- Areas of agreement.
- Areas of disagreement.
- Evidence strength.
- Unresolved questions.
- AI uncertainty notes.
- Human review status when available.
