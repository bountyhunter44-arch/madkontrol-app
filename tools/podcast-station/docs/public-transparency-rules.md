# Public Transparency Rules

## Purpose

Public trust requires making AI involvement visible, understandable, and contestable.

## Required Public Labels

Public episodes or summaries should disclose:

- Which participants were human.
- Which participants were AI.
- Which AI profiles were used.
- Whether summaries were AI-generated, human-edited, or both.
- Whether sources were verified.
- Whether moderation actions affected the public output.
- Whether corrections were made after publication.

## Summary Transparency

Every public summary should include:

- Method note.
- Confidence note.
- Missing context note.
- Disagreement preservation note.
- Correction pathway.

## Source Transparency

AI must distinguish between:

- Provided source.
- Recalled general knowledge.
- Inference.
- Participant claim.
- Unverified statement.
- Moderator judgment.

## Correction Rules

Corrections should be visible and timestamped in a future implementation. They should explain:

- What changed.
- Why it changed.
- Whether the change affects the summary, transcript, or moderation label.

## Public Trust Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- Hidden AI participants.
- Overconfident summaries.
- Removing disagreement for readability.
- Publishing source-free claims as facts.
- Presenting compromise as moral superiority.
- Making moderation outcomes opaque.
