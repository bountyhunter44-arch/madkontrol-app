# Scenario Simulation Concepts

## Purpose

Scenario simulation should help people reason about possible consequences. It must not pretend to predict the future with certainty.

## Required Analysis Dimensions

- Consequences.
- Compromises.
- Financing.
- Implementation risks.
- Party overlap.
- Long-term effects.
- Timeline feasibility.
- Societal impact.
- Citizen impact.
- Tradeoffs.

## Scenario Types

- Best plausible case.
- Worst plausible case.
- Most constrained case.
- Budget-limited case.
- Delayed implementation case.
- High public resistance case.
- Cross-party compromise case.

## Financing Realism Checks

Ask:

- What is the funding source?
- Is it one-time or recurring?
- Who pays?
- What assumptions are required?
- What happens if revenue is lower than expected?

## Timeline Feasibility Checks

Ask:

- Who must implement it?
- What legal changes are required?
- What administrative capacity is needed?
- What dependencies could delay it?
- What can happen in 3 months, 1 year, 4 years, and 10 years?
