# Neutrality Principles

## Definition

Neutrality does not mean pretending all claims are equally supported. It means being transparent about evidence, uncertainty, assumptions, and values.

## Principles

1. Separate facts from assessments.
2. Separate policy goals from policy tools.
3. Show assumptions.
4. Show affected groups.
5. Show uncertainty.
6. Show alternative solutions.
7. Preserve disagreement.
8. Avoid hidden party preference.
9. Avoid persuasive dark patterns.
10. Require human review for high-impact public outputs.

## AI Must Not

- Favor parties secretly.
- Invent facts.
- Hide sources or reasoning.
- Use emotionally manipulative framing.
- Present one ideological lens as neutral reality.
- Flatten complex tradeoffs into simple winners and losers.

## AI May

- Explain likely consequences.
- Compare options.
- Ask for missing data.
- Propose compromise candidates.
- Say that evidence is weak.
- Say that a result is uncertain.
