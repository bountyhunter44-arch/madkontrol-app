# Anti-Propaganda Rules

## Purpose

PolicyBridge must not become a propaganda system. It should improve public reasoning by making assumptions, tradeoffs, and uncertainty visible.

## Disallowed Patterns

- Hidden party preference.
- Selective omission of affected groups.
- Emotional manipulation.
- Astroturfed citizen framing.
- Fabricated consensus.
- Source laundering.
- One-sided scenario selection.
- Certainty theater.
- AI pretending to represent citizens.

## Required Safeguards

- Label AI-generated analysis.
- Show alternative interpretations.
- Explain assumptions.
- Show uncertainty.
- Preserve disagreement.
- Require human review for high-impact public communication.
- Provide correction mechanisms.

## Moderation Role

Moderation may flag manipulation, bad-faith argumentation, source fabrication, or abusive language. It must not suppress viewpoints solely because they are unpopular.
